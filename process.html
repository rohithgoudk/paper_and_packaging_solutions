The four stages

One workflow, zero handoffs.

01 / Design

Structure & art

Our structural designers model your box to the product, then our art team lays out the print file around it.

02 / Prototype

Sample & sign-off

A physical white or print-accurate sample ships to you within 48 hours for approval before tooling.

03 / Produce

Short or long run

From 250-unit digital runs to 500,000-unit flexo orders, produced across our six facilities.

04 / Deliver

Palletized & tracked

Freight booked, palletized, and tracked to your warehouse or 3PL — with reorder points set for next time.

Week by week

What happens between quote and pallet.

01

Day 1 — Structural model

You send dimensions or a sample. Our team returns a structural die-line and cost estimate within one business day.

02

Day 2-3 — White sample

An uncut, unprinted sample ships so you can check fit and fold before any art is finalized.

03

Day 4-9 — Print-accurate proof

Once art is approved, a production-matched proof ships for final sign-off on color and finish.

04

Day 10+ — Production & freight

Your run is scheduled at the facility with the right press and flute for your volume, then palletized and booked to your dock.

Quality assurance

Tested before it ever reaches a belt.

Every structural design goes through drop, compression, and vibration testing before a single production unit is cut, so the box that ships is the box that survives transit.

  • Edge crush and burst strength testing
  • Simulated freight vibration & drop tests
  • Color match verified against your proof
